Brutalism was honest. The building told you what it was made of and where it was going. We stopped making buildings like that.

The buildings that last — still interesting fifty years on — are the ones that didn't try to flatter. They argued with their site, with the sun. They won some arguments. They lost others visibly. That visible loss is what makes them worth looking at.
